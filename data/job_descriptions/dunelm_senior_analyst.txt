ABOUT THE COMPANY

Dunelm is the UK’s number one homewares retailer, offering around 100,000 products through a multichannel retail business. The company is investing heavily in supply chain, stores, technology, and digital capabilities to improve the customer experience and support long-term growth.

As part of its broader Data & Insight transformation, Dunelm is growing its Analytics team to put data and insight at the heart of decision-making across the business.


ROLE OVERVIEW

Dunelm is hiring a Senior Analyst to join the Customer and Performance Analytics team.

This role focuses on understanding customer behaviour across stores and digital channels, as well as competitor performance. You will generate insights and recommendations that inform business strategy, with a particular emphasis on customer experience, performance reporting, and continuous improvement.


KEY RESPONSIBILITIES

- Support weekly trade reporting so stakeholders are informed on customer performance
- Deliver data-driven insights on business performance drivers and trading decisions
- Collaborate across the Insights & Analytics team to share key findings and business context
- Build deep understanding of Dunelm’s CSAT survey programme, including methodology and reporting
- Work closely with the Research team to combine customer research with quantitative data analysis
- Develop and maintain dashboards, reports, and presentations for CSAT and customer performance
- Partner with Data Product and Engineering teams to define internal and external data needs
- Act as an advocate for data, insight, and the voice of the customer across the business


CANDIDATE PROFILE

- Strong analytical skills with experience solving business problems using data
- Preferably some retail or consumer-focused experience
- Strong commercial awareness and business acumen
- Excellent stakeholder management and communication skills
- Ability to synthesise insight from multiple sources into a clear narrative and actionable recommendations
- Comfortable in fast-paced environments and adaptable to changing priorities
- Experience or interest in quantitative research methodologies is a plus
